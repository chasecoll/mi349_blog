Welcome Pandemic Pals

Author: Chase Colligan

Date Published: 2020-9-11

Hello fellow quarantine buddies! Welcome to the Quarantine Chronicles, a blog where I post about how I'm trying to improve my life while in a pandemic world. I'm sure plenty of you can relate, so follow along and see what I get up to. Today I'm figuring out how to workout. On most days I would just hop in my car and drive to the gym for a quick workout. But the gym I used to go to is closed and will not be reopening. As a result, I'm stuck at home wondering how I'm going to keep myself in shape. If I'm quarantined how am I supposed to exercise, there's nowhere to go. I felt a little panicked about it for a while until I found an instagram post on calisthenics. I looked more into it and found that calisthenics is very easy to start up. The whole idea of calisthenics is to use your bodyweight to build muscle. That includes exercises such as,

  • Pushups
  • Squats
  • Pull Ups
  • Situps
and much much more. With simple exercises such as these working out should be a breeze. I should be able to knockout a quick circuit in 30 - 45 minutes. So for the rest of the week I am going to do my best to stay consistent with my workouts. That way at the end of the week I can look back on the week and see if I progressed. Tune in next week for my results on calisthenics!

Use It Again

Author: Chase Colligan

Date Published: 2020-10-11

If it's not broken don't throw it away. That was the phrase that I heard quite often while growing up. My grandfather was a naturalist, which meant that nature conservation was drilled into my head from a young age. He would reuse anything and everything that he could. Mason jars were cups, boxes were used as flooring, and tennis balls were chair leg coverings. Everything that I thought was trash never was. Now I have moved into a new house with new roomates. These roomates evidently did not have a grandfather who was as passionate about conservation as mine was. They throw everything away and barely recycle. So this week I am going to do my best to push recycling, composting, and overall respect for the planet more on to my roomates. Because the planet needs help. There is a pandemic going around taking over the news sources, but it's not the only issue. Climate change is going to do more damage to the planet unless we decide to do something about it. Reusing simple household items is a good start, but it's not enough. The more everyone does to help support the planet, the better it will be. Overall it starts with one, so why can't the one be me. Let's clean up the planet and make everything safer for us!
